About


River Dell High School's First-ever Hackathon!

Hack River Dell, is the first ever hackathon hosted at River Dell Regional High School in Oradell, New Jersey. Organized entirely by students, the Hack River Dell team looks to continue the tradition of excellence and innovation that River Dell High School has been recognized for for generations.

Local Hack Day 2016

Hack River Dell is honored to be recognized as one of Major League Hacking’s sites for Local Hack Day 2016. Schools from around the globe simultaneously host their own local hackathon, making Local Hack Day the single largest day of student hacking ever.

Major League Hacking

Major League Hacking (MLH) is the official student hackathon league. Each year, MLH powers over 200 weekend-long invention competitions that inspire innovation, cultivate communities and teach computer science skills to more than 65,000 students around the world. MLH is an engaged and passionate maker community, consisting of the next generation of technology leaders and entrepreneurs. Read more at mlh.io/about

FAQ


What's a Hackathon?

By definition, a hackathon is a coding event in which teams “hack” together to create an awesome project like a game, website, or app! Hackathons are a great way to learn more about coding, meet new people, and build something cool.


How long is Hack River Dell '16?

The event has a 12-hour runtime, and will go from 9 AM to 9 PM. Keep in mind that there is also and opening and closing ceremony before and after the event. Check in begins at 7:30 AM, and the closing ceremony will end at 10:30 PM. For the full event schedule, please refer to the event schedule.


Who can attend?

This event is open for all middle through high school students (Grades 7-12), no matter where they live or what institution they are enrolled to.


What if I don't know how to code?

No problem! The purpose of a hackathon is to get everyone involved, and we especially encourage beginners to attend. Hackathons are a great place to come and learn programming for the first time.


How do teams work?

At Hack River Dell, you can either come and hack own your own, or come with a team. If you want to work with a team, but don’t one have one, we can help you find one on the day of the event.



What should I bring?

You should bring your laptop or desktop, school ID, phone, charging cables, and any hardware you plan on using. Power strips and extension cords should be brought as well.


When can I start my hack?

Hacks must begin after the opening ceremony in order to qualify for judging. Anyone who starts their hack before 9 AM will be disqualified.


How much does it cost to attend?

There is no fee to attend and participate at the event. Even food will be provided free of charge. However, if attending the event requires you to take mass transit, we are unable to reimburse your expenses. If you need to take mass transit, please inquire here.


How and when do I submit my hack?

Submissions begin at 9:00 PM promptly, and are open until 9:15 PM. You can, however, continue to work on your presentation or your hack after 9:00 PM. We will send out instructions on how to submit your hack at least 3 hours before submission time.


What do I need for the expo?

You should have a PowerPoint presentation, some sort of multimedia presentation (e.g. a Prezi), or some sort of demonstration of your hack available for use during the expo and if you are selected as a finalist by the judges.
