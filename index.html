About


River Dell High School's First-ever Hackathon

Hack River Dell, is the first ever hackathon hosted at River Dell Regional High School in Oradell, New Jersey. Organized entirely by students, the Hack River Dell team looks to continue the tradition of excellence and innovation that River Dell High School has been recognized for for generations.

Win cool tech prizes and free stuff

Throughout the day there will be plenty of opportunities to win free t-shirts, hats, notebooks, water bottles, and other fun stuff. Hacks will be judged based on originality, innovativeness, and execution. Each team has a shot at winning cool tech prizes including the grand prize! (It's a good prize, we promise)

Local Hack Day 2016

Hack River Dell is honored to be recognized as one of Major League Hacking’s sites for Local Hack Day 2016 hosted by GitHub. Schools from around the globe simultaneously host their own local hackathon, making Local Hack Day the single largest day of student hacking ever. Read more at localhackday.mlh.io

Major League Hacking

Major League Hacking (MLH) is the official student hackathon league. Each year, MLH powers over 200 weekend-long invention competitions that inspire innovation, cultivate communities and teach computer science skills to more than 65,000 students around the world. MLH is an engaged and passionate maker community, consisting of the next generation of technology leaders and entrepreneurs. Read more at mlh.io/about

FAQ


What's a Hackathon?

A hackathon is a coding event in which teams “hack” together to create an awesome project like a game, website, or app! Hackathons are a great way to learn more about coding, meet new people, and build something cool. Check out "What is a hackathon" by Dave Fontenot to learn more


How long is Hack River Dell '16?

The event has a 12-hour runtime in which teams can work on their hacks, and that will run from 9 AM to 9 PM. Keep in mind that there is also and opening and closing ceremony before and after the event. Check in begins at 7:30 AM, and the closing ceremony will end at 10:30 PM. For the full event schedule, please refer to the event schedule below.


Who can attend?

This event is open for all middle through high school students (Grades 7-12), no matter where they live or what school they attend.


What if I don't know how to code?

No problem! The purpose of a hackathon is to get everyone involved, and we especially encourage beginners to attend. Hackathons are a great place to come and learn programming for the first time. A great example of a hack that didn't require any code is Notis.


How do teams work?

At Hack River Dell, you can either come and hack own your own, or come with a team. Teams may consist of no more than 5 people per team. If you want to work with a team, but don’t one have one, we can help you find one on the day of the event.



What should I bring?

You need to bring a laptop or desktop computer, school ID, phone, charging cables, powerstrips, and any hardware you plan on using. Also, do not forget to bring the signed waiver, this will be emailed to you if you register.


When can I start my hack?

Hacking begins after the opening ceremony at 9 AM in order to qualify for judging. Anyone who starts their hack before 9 AM will be disqualified.


How much does it cost to attend?

Nothing! Hack River Dell it totally free! There is no fee to attend and participate at the event. Even food and merchandise will be provided free of charge.


How and when do I submit my hack?

Submissions begin at 9:00 PM promptly, and are open until 9:15 PM. You can, however, continue to work on your presentation or your hack after 9:00 PM. We will send out instructions on how to submit your hack at least 3 hours before submission time.


What do I need for the expo?

You should have some sort of demonstration of your hack during the expo (e.g. PowerPoint presentation, hands-on demo, or whatever is applicable for your hack).

Gurbir S. Grewal


   Gurbir S. Grewal was appointed Acting Bergen County Prosecutor on January 4, 2016 and oversees a staff of approximately 265 personnel at the Bergen County Prosecutor’s Office (“BCPO”), consisting of Assistant Prosecutors, Detectives, and support staff. He also exercises supervisory authority over approximately 2,700 sworn law enforcement officers in 72 law enforcement agencies that operate in Bergen County. In addition to conducting its own proactive investigations, the BCPO maintains specialized squads that assist municipal police departments in the investigation of crimes calling for increased personnel, specialized equipment, technical know-how, or highly specialized training. These crimes include arson, homicide, sex crimes, drug trafficking, traditional organized crime, domestic violence, financial crimes, and cybercrimes.

   Prior to his appointment, Acting Prosecutor Grewal worked as an Assistant United States Attorney (“AUSA”) in the Criminal Division of the United States Attorney’s Office for the District of New Jersey from 2010 to 2016. While at the U.S. Attorney’s Office, he served as Chief of the Economic Crimes Unit from 2014 to 2016 and oversaw the investigation and prosecution of all major white collar and cybercrimes in the District of New Jersey.

   Before becoming a federal prosecutor in New Jersey, Acting Prosecutor Grewal also served as an AUSA in the Criminal Division of the United States Attorney’s Office for the Eastern District of New York from 2004 to 2007, where he was ultimately assigned to the Business and Securities Fraud Unit.

   In addition to his work as a federal prosecutor, Acting Prosecutor Grewal has also worked in private practice, including at Howrey LLP (from 1999 to 2004 in Washington, D.C., and from 2008 to 2010 in New York, NY. He counseled clients on a range of matters including securities, trademark, antitrust and FCPA issues; represented individuals and companies in government investigations and criminal proceedings; conducted internal investigations for public corporations; and conducted civil trials.

   Acting Prosecutor Grewal graduated cum laude with a Bachelor of Science in Foreign Service from the Georgetown University School of Foreign Service in 1995. He obtained his law degree from the College of William & Mary, Marshall-Wythe School of Law in 1999.

David Weinstein


   Dave Weinstein is the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) for the State of New Jersey, where he serves as a member of Governor Chris Christie’s cabinet. As CTO, Dave leads the New Jersey Office of Information Technology.

   Prior to this June 2016 appointment, Dave previously served as New Jersey’s Chief Information Security Officer and Cybersecurity Advisor with the New Jersey Office of Homeland Security and Preparedness. Outside of Trenton, Dave was a senior civilian at the United States Cyber Command in Fort Meade, Maryland, as well as a cyber risk consultant with Deloitte and Touche.

   Dave has been recognized by Forbes as a “top 20 cyber policy expert” and his analysis and commentary has been featured in numerous media and academic publications, including the Georgetown Journal of International Affairs, Foreign Affairs, Foreign Policy, CNN.com, The Boston Globe, and The Huffington Post.

   Dave is also a Cybersecurity Fellow with New America and an “Influencer” for the Christian Science Monitor’s security and privacy project. He is the author of the forthcoming chapter “Information Sharing at the State and Local Level,” published in Cyber Insecurity: Navigating the Perils of the Information Age (Rowan & Littlefield Publishers, Inc.).

   A native of Westfield, New Jersey, Dave earned his Bachelor’s degree from Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore and his Master’s degree from Georgetown University in Washington, DC.

Judson Welle


   L. Judson Welle is an Assistant United States Attorney assigned to the Economic Crimes Unit in the Criminal Division of the U.S. Attorney’s Office for the District of New Jersey. His experience encompasses cases involving computer hacking, international terrorism, economic espionage, theft of trade secrets, insider threats, critical infrastructure protection, and counter-proliferation.

   Mr. Welle also serves as Computer Hacking and Intellectual Property Coordinator and National Security Cyber Specialist for the U.S. Attorney’s Office. In these roles, Mr. Welle coordinates the Office’s work prosecuting computer intrusion cases and provides guidance to other prosecutors on gathering digital evidence and complying with privacy protections.

   Prior to becoming a federal prosecutor in April 2005, Mr. Welle worked in the New York office of Dewey Ballantine LLP on internal investigations and complex civil and criminal cases. He also served as a law clerk to the Honorable John W. Bissell, Chief District Judge, United States District Court for the District of New Jersey.

   Mr. Welle graduated from Rutgers University in 1996 with a B.A. in Political Science and Psychology and was awarded a J.D. in 2000 by the William and Mary School of Law, where he served as Editor-in-Chief of the law review. Mr. Welle is a member of bar in New York and New Jersey.
